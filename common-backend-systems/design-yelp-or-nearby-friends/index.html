1. Basic Knowledge And Solution

What This Question Is Testing

Yelp (or Nearby Friends) is geospatial search: "find places (or friends) within R of me, filtered by category, ranked by relevance." The hard part is indexing millions of locations, supporting sub-second queries, ranking by review quality, and handling updates (new reviews, business changes).

The invariant is this:

Every business in the catalog is discoverable by geographic query and category filter; reviews are durably stored, attributed to authors, and aggregated to per-business ratings; spam reviews are filtered; opening hours and special status (closed, claimed) reflect ground truth.

For Nearby Friends: every user's current location is privately shared with chosen friends; friends within R receive a presence indicator; location data is ephemeral.

At ICT5 / L6+ level, the interviewer is testing whether you can design geospatial indexing (geohash, S2, H3), business and review data models, ranking algorithms, photo/media handling, and reason about privacy in location-sharing.

60-Second Interview Framing

Spoken version:

I would design Yelp around a geospatial business index, a review service with spam filtering and per-business aggregates, and a media service for photos. Geospatial queries use H3 or geohash cells. Search combines geo filter with category and ranking signals. For Nearby Friends, location is ephemeral and per-friend consent-based; the same geo index serves both with different access models. The interesting decisions are geo indexing, ranking, review aggregation, and privacy.

Requirements To Clarify First

Functional requirements:

  • Search businesses by location and category.
  • View business detail: hours, photos, reviews, ratings.
  • Post a review with rating and photos.
  • Check in (Yelp); presence (Nearby Friends).
  • Filter by price, rating, distance.
  • Photos, menus, reservations (out of scope for core).
  • Business claim and management.

Non-functional requirements:

  • Sub-second search.
  • Petabyte-scale photo storage.
  • Review write throughput moderate; read very high.
  • Ratings update reflected within seconds.
  • Multi-region.

Out of scope unless asked:

  • Reservation system (separate).
  • Delivery integration.
  • Ad targeting on reviews.

Geospatial Indexing

H3 (or S2) hexagonal cell hierarchy:

  • Each location maps to a cell at every resolution.
  • Resolution 9 ≈ 100m, resolution 8 ≈ 250m, etc.
  • For "find businesses within 1 km of lat/lng": compute cells covering the disc; query each cell's business set.

Index storage:

  • Per-cell list of business_ids.
  • In-memory for hot regions; persistent backing for cold.
  • Updates: business location change moves it between cells.

For category-aware queries: index by (cell, category) so "coffee shops within 1 km" is a fast lookup.

Interview phrase:

H3 cells turn "nearby" into "in these cells." Then filter and rank.

Search Flow

For "best italian restaurants in SoMa, San Francisco":

  1. Geocode location (SoMa SF) to lat/lng or cells.
  2. Geo filter: businesses in cells covering target area.
  3. Category filter: italian.
  4. Other filters: price, rating, currently open.
  5. Rank: by relevance score combining distance, rating, review count, popularity, personalization.
  6. Return top N with snippet (name, rating, photo, distance).

Latency budget: under 500ms. Most is spent on: - Cell scans: tens of ms. - Filter and rank: hundreds of ms (depending on result set size). - Photo URL hydration: tens of ms.

Ranking

Multi-signal ranking:

  • Distance.
  • Average rating (Bayesian-adjusted for low review count).
  • Review count (more reviews = more reliable).
  • Recency of reviews.
  • Photo quality.
  • Personalization (user's past visits, preferences).
  • Sponsored boost (paid placement).

Multi-stage: cheap geo + category filter → broad candidate set; ML ranker scores top candidates; return.

Review Aggregation

When a review is posted:

  1. Persist review.
  2. Update business rating_avg and review_count (eventually consistent).
  3. Emit event for spam detection async.
  4. If flagged, mark spam_status=suspicious; aggregate recomputes excluding it.

Rating aggregate:

  • Stored on business record: rating_avg, review_count.
  • Updated by an aggregator service that consumes review events.
  • Sub-counter sharding if a single hot business gets a surge.

Bayesian smoothing:

  • A business with 1 review of 5 stars shouldn't rank above a business with 100 reviews averaging 4.8.
  • Adjusted rating = (avg * count + prior * confidence) / (count + confidence).

Spam And Fake Reviews

Continuous battle.

Signals:

  • Reviewer's account age and history.
  • IP/device patterns (many reviews from one IP).
  • Text patterns (templated, suspicious phrasing).
  • Rating anomalies (5-star clusters in short timeframes).
  • Competitor 1-star bombs.

Pipeline:

  • Async ML scoring of each review.
  • High-confidence spam removed automatically.
  • Borderline goes to manual review.
  • Suspicious reviews excluded from aggregate but visible (or hidden) per policy.

Photos

  • Uploaded via separate upload pipeline.
  • Stored in object storage; thumbnails generated async.
  • CDN-served for views.
  • Moderation: automated classifier + human review for flagged.

Nearby Friends

Separate plane on top of the same geospatial index.

  • User opts in; sets visibility duration.
  • Client periodically (~30 seconds) sends location to server.
  • Server stores in-memory with TTL.
  • Per-friend ACL: who can see this user's location.
  • Friends within R receive presence in their app.

Privacy:

  • Always opt-in.
  • Time-bounded (e.g., "share for 1 hour").
  • Per-friend granularity.
  • Location resolution may be coarsened (city level instead of street).
  • Audit logs.

Failure Modes And Mitigations

Failure mode Mitigation Signal to monitor
Spam flood. ML filter + manual review; rate limits. Suspicious rate spike.
Business location wrong. Owner correction; map provider integration. Geo dispute count.
Aggregate lag. Recompute from reviews. Aggregate-vs-recount drift.
Photo abuse. Moderation pipeline. Reports.
Search latency. Cache popular queries; backpressure. Search p99.
Friend privacy leak. Strict ACL; audit; bug bounty. Privacy reports.
Geocoder error. Multiple providers; fallback. Geocode failure rate.
Hot business (gone viral). Cache aggregate; sub-counter for likes/visits. Per-business QPS.
Closed business still shown. Status flag; periodic verification. Closed-but-visible count.
Multi-region replication lag. Async; bounded. Replication lag.

Operational Metrics

Correctness:

  • Spam detection rate; false positives.
  • Aggregate drift.
  • Geocoder accuracy.

Capacity:

  • Search QPS by region.
  • Review write rate.
  • Photo egress.

Business:

  • Reviews per business; user contribution.
  • Conversion (view → visit).
  • Claim rate.

Multi-Region Strategy

  • Per-region geo index for local search.
  • Business catalog replicated globally.
  • Reviews region-local for writes; replicated to global aggregate.
  • Photos served via CDN.

Tradeoffs To Call Out

  • Bayesian smoothing trades responsiveness for fairness in new businesses.
  • Aggressive spam filtering reduces noise but hits legitimate negative reviews.
  • Detailed personalization improves relevance but raises privacy concerns.
  • Caching aggregates speeds reads but adds invalidation.
  • Higher resolution geo cells give precision but cost memory.
  • Real-time location sharing adds load; coarsening reduces precision.

2. Simulated Interview

Interviewer: Design Yelp.

Candidate: Geospatial business index plus review service plus photo service, all queried by a composite search service. The invariant is every business is discoverable by location and category; reviews aggregate correctly to per-business ratings; spam is filtered.

Interviewer: Geo indexing?

Candidate: H3 hexagonal cells. Each business maps to a cell at a fine resolution (~100m). Search by location: compute cells covering the disc around the target, query each cell's business set, filter by category and other criteria, rank, return. For category-aware queries, index by (cell, category) to skip the filter step.

Interviewer: Ranking?

Candidate: Multi-signal. Distance from query point, Bayesian-smoothed rating, review count, recency, popularity, personalization. Multi-stage: cheap geo + category filter produces broad candidate set; ML ranker scores top candidates. Bayesian smoothing prevents a 1-review business with 5 stars from outranking a 100-review business at 4.8.

Interviewer: Reviews — how do you handle aggregates?

Candidate: Per-business rating_avg and review_count stored denormalized. Aggregator service consumes review events and updates these. Eventually consistent — a fresh review may take seconds to reflect. For hot businesses (newly viral), sub-counter sharding handles the write rate. Periodic full recount from reviews keeps aggregates honest.

Interviewer: Spam reviews?

Candidate: Async ML pipeline. Signals: account age, IP/device clustering, text patterns, rating clusters, competitor 1-star bombs. High-confidence spam removed automatically. Borderline goes to manual review. Suspicious reviews excluded from aggregate but may still be visible to user (or hidden per policy). The pipeline runs after the post path so reviews appear quickly; if flagged later, aggregates adjust.

Interviewer: A user is in SoMa SF and searches "italian." What happens?

Candidate: Frontend geocodes "SoMa" or uses user GPS. Search service computes H3 cells covering ~1-2 km around the target. Query each cell's (cell, category=italian) index. Get candidate businesses. Filter by other criteria (open now, price, rating). Rank by composite score. Hydrate top results with photo, snippet. Return. Sub-second total.

Interviewer: A photo is uploaded with a review.

Candidate: Photo uploaded via separate media service; signed URL to object storage. Thumbnail generated async. Moderation runs (automated + human if flagged). Photo associated with the review and business. Served via CDN.

Interviewer: Nearby Friends — design it.

Candidate: Separate plane on top of the same geospatial index, but with strict privacy. User opts in and sets visibility duration ("share for 1 hour"). Client sends location every ~30 seconds. Server stores in-memory with TTL. Per-friend ACL controls who can see whose location. Friends within R receive a presence indicator. Location can be coarsened (city level) per user preference. Audit logs of accesses.

Interviewer: Privacy concerns with Nearby Friends?

Candidate: Several. Opt-in only; default off. Time-bounded sharing. Per-friend granularity (different friends see different precision). Coarsening at the server. No long-term retention; TTL'd. Strict ACL enforcement. Audit logs accessible to user. Bug bounty for any leakage. We treat location as the most sensitive data.

Interviewer: Multi-region?

Candidate: Per-region geo index — businesses local to each region. Business catalog replicated globally for canonical info. Reviews region-local for writes; replicated. Aggregates per region with global rollup. Photos via CDN everywhere. Friend locations are ephemeral and region-local.

Interviewer: A business has 5 stars but only 2 reviews. Should it rank high?

Candidate: No. Bayesian smoothing handles this. Effective rating = (5 * 2 + prior * confidence) / (2 + confidence). With prior=3.5 and confidence=10, this gives (10 + 35) / 12 = 3.75, well below an established 4.8-rated business. As the business accumulates reviews, the smoothing weakens.

Interviewer: Summarize.

Candidate: H3-cell geo index plus business catalog plus review service plus photos. Search filters by location and category, ranks by Bayesian-smoothed multi-signal score, hydrates results. Reviews aggregate eventually; spam filtered async. Nearby Friends rides on the same index with strict privacy: opt-in, TTL, ACL, coarsening. Multi-region with per-region indexes and global catalog. The hardest correctness problem is review spam; the hardest scaling problem is hot business aggregates.

One-Minute Closeout

Yelp is geospatial search with ranked reviews. H3 cells make nearby fast; Bayesian-smoothed ratings handle low-review businesses; multi-signal ranking blends distance, popularity, personalization. Reviews aggregate eventually; spam filtering is async ML. Photos via CDN. Nearby Friends uses the same geo index with strict privacy: opt-in, ephemeral, per-friend ACL.
